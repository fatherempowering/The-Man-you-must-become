Chapter 01 / 08

Protein

The strength you owe your family.

After 40, you lose between 1 and 2% of muscle mass per year if you don't actively fight it. That's not an abstract statistic. It's the difference between the father who picks up his kids and the one who makes excuses not to.

Protein is the only macronutrient that stops this decline. Not partially — completely, if you consume enough of it.

Why protein defines your physique

Proteins are made of amino acids — the building blocks of your muscles, hormones, and immune system. Nine of these amino acids are essential: your body cannot produce them. You must get them from food.

Without sufficient intake, your body goes looking for amino acids elsewhere. Where? In your muscle tissue. This is called catabolism. At 40, with naturally declining testosterone, your body is even more vulnerable to this process.

Key Fact

Leucine, found in abundance in meats, eggs, and dairy, directly triggers muscle protein synthesis. 2 to 3g of leucine per meal is enough to activate this process.

How much protein per day?

The science-backed recommendation for an active man between 38 and 49:

  • Transformation and fat loss: 0.9 to 1.1g per lb of bodyweight
  • Muscle maintenance: 0.7 to 0.9g per lb
  • Example: 185 lbs = 165 to 205g of protein per day

These numbers are intentionally higher than general recommendations. After 40, the efficiency of protein synthesis decreases. You need more to get the same result you got at 25.

Best protein sources

  • Chicken breast : 31g per 100g
  • Whole eggs : complete source, maximum bioavailability
  • Salmon : 25g per 100g, plus anti-inflammatory omega-3s
  • Lean ground beef : 26g per 100g, plus zinc and B12
  • Cottage cheese / Greek yogurt : 10 to 17g, ideal at night
Field Rule

Build every meal around a protein source. 30 to 40g per meal, 4 times a day. Everything else on the plate follows that anchor.

Carbohydrates

Fuel for action. Not for passivity.

Carbohydrates are not your enemy. Passivity is your enemy. An active father who trains hard needs carbs. A sedentary man on the couch, not as much.

The question isn't whether to eliminate carbs. The question is whether you've earned them.

How carbs work

Carbohydrates are converted into glucose, which fuels your muscles during intense effort. They're stored as glycogen in your muscles and liver. When your reserves are full, you have the energy to train hard. When they're depleted, your performance collapses.

The problem for most men over 40 isn't eating too many carbs. It's consuming the wrong carbs, at the wrong time, without burning them off.

The Principle

Low-glycemic carbs (sweet potatoes, oats, brown rice) keep your energy stable. Refined carbs create insulin spikes followed by energy crashes, and promote fat storage.

How much for you?

Training Days

  • 1.4 to 2.3g per lb of bodyweight
  • Ex. 185 lbs = 260 to 425g

Rest Days

  • 0.7 to 1.1g per lb of bodyweight
  • Ex. 185 lbs = 130 to 205g

Recommended sources

  • Sweet potato, nutrient-dense, moderate glycemic index
  • White rice around training, fast digestion, ideal pre/post workout
  • Oats, slow-digesting, perfect for the morning
  • Fruit, natural sugars plus micronutrients
  • Green vegetables, fiber, volume, nutrients — unlimited
The question isn't whether to eliminate carbs.
The question is whether you've earned them.
Chapter 03 / 08

Fats

The hormonal foundation you cannot ignore.

Testosterone is built from cholesterol. Cholesterol comes from dietary fat. A diet too low in fat means low testosterone, which means less energy, less muscle, less drive.

Eating healthy fats is not optional for a man over 40. It is a biological necessity.

The fats you need to eat

  • Monounsaturated fats. Olive oil, avocados, almonds. Anti-inflammatory. Support cardiovascular health and your hormonal profile.
  • Omega-3s (polyunsaturated fats). Salmon, sardines, walnuts, chia seeds. Reduce systemic inflammation, the same inflammation that slows your recovery and sabotages your training.
  • Saturated fats (in moderation). Grass-fed beef, whole eggs. A moderate intake of saturated fats from whole food sources supports testosterone production.
How Much?

20 to 35% of your total daily calories.

For a target of 2,500 calories per day:
55 to 97g of fat per day.

Watch Out

Avoid trans fats found in processed foods. If the label says "partially hydrogenated", put it back.

Chapter 04 / 08

Micronutrients

The secret weapons of male performance.

Vitamins and minerals don't directly provide energy. But without them, every other system runs at 60% capacity. You train hard, you eat well, and you recover poorly because a magnesium deficiency is destroying your sleep.

The 4 critical micronutrients

  • Vitamin D : The testosterone vitamin
    The majority of men in the northern US are deficient. Directly linked to testosterone production and muscle strength. Target: 2,000 to 4,000 IU per day. Pair with K2 (100mcg).
  • Zinc : Testosterone and recovery
    Deficiency equals low testosterone. Sources: oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds. Supplement if your diet is low in red meat.
  • Magnesium : Sleep and recovery
    Improves sleep quality, reduces cramping, manages cortisol. Sources: spinach, nuts, pumpkin seeds, 85%+ dark chocolate.
  • Omega-3s (EPA/DHA)
    Anti-inflammatory. Joint recovery, brain, heart. 1 to 3g of EPA+DHA per day. Fatty fish 3x per week or supplement.
Simple Strategy

Eat vegetables of 3 different colors every day. That single principle covers the majority of your micronutrient needs.

Meal Timing

The structure of a disciplined man.

A father without structure doesn't eat poorly by choice. He eats poorly by default. Meal timing isn't an obsession — it's a system that removes decisions from the equation.

The daily framework

  • Meal 1 — Breakfast (the launch signal)
    High protein. Complex carbs. Healthy fats. This meal sets your energy level for the next 4 hours.
    Eggs + oats + avocado · Greek yogurt + fruit + nuts.
  • Dinner (recovery signal)
    Protein-focused with quality carbs. Fuel tonight's recovery so tomorrow's performance is protected.
    Salmon + sweet potato + spinach · Ground beef + rice + broccoli.
  • Post-workout (within 2 hours)
    Fast protein plus carbs to reload glycogen. This is the rebuild window. Don't skip it.
  • Evening meal
    Slow-digesting protein plus vegetables plus fats. Supports overnight recovery.
    Cottage cheese + berries · Salmon + asparagus + olive oil.
On Intermittent Fasting

The 16:8 protocol is compatible with this system. Condition: hit your protein targets within the 8-hour window.

Chapter 06 / 08

Hydration

The most overlooked performance tool.

A dehydration of just 2% of bodyweight measurably reduces physical performance. Focus, strength, endurance — everything drops. And most men show up to their training sessions already dehydrated without knowing it.

Your daily requirements

  • Baseline for active men: 125 oz (about one gallon) per day
  • Add 16 to 32 oz per hour of intense training
  • Simple indicator: clear to pale yellow urine = well hydrated

Electrolytes: why water alone isn't enough

Sodium, potassium, and magnesium regulate fluid balance inside your cells. During intense sweating, you lose them. Without replacing them: cramps, fatigue, and performance collapse.

Simple Solution

32 oz of water + 1/4 tsp sea salt + juice of one lemon. Effective, cheap, zero additives.

The Meal Prep
System

Discipline built into your nutrition.

Improvising your meals is the single biggest threat to your transformation. When you're drained after a long day, your brain defaults to easy. If easy means pizza, that's what you eat. If easy means prepped chicken and cooked rice in the fridge, that's what you eat.

Meal prep is not a constraint. It is an act of leadership toward yourself.

The 4-step system

01

Plan on Sunday (20 min)

3 to 4 protein sources for the week. 2 complex carb sources. Your grocery list. That's it.

02

Prep Sunday evening (90 min)

Batch cook your proteins. Cook your carbs. Wash and cut your vegetables. Portion everything into containers.

03

Assemble, don't cook

Monday through Friday: open the fridge, grab a portion from each category. 5 minutes per meal.

04

Iterate every week

What's left on Friday tells you what to adjust the following week.

Sample weekly setup

Proteins & Carbs

  • Chicken, salmon, hard-boiled eggs, tuna
  • Rice, sweet potatoes, oats

Vegetables & Fats

  • Broccoli, spinach, bell peppers
  • Avocados, olive oil, mixed nuts

Supplements

What actually works — without the marketing.

The supplement industry is a $180 billion market built on exaggerated promises. But some supplements have solid scientific backing and real value for an active father over 40.

What Works

  • Creatine Monohydrate
    The most studied supplement in existence. Increases strength, improves recovery. 3 to 5g daily. ~$10 to $15/month.
  • Whey Protein
    A convenience tool. Useful if you can't hit your protein targets with food alone. Choose whey isolate.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2
    Essential for most men in northern states. 2,000 to 4,000 IU of D3 with 100mcg K2. Take with a fat-containing meal.
  • Magnesium Glycinate
    Sleep, recovery, stress management. 300 to 400mg before bed.
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)
    If you don't eat fatty fish 3x per week. 1 to 2g of EPA+DHA daily.

Skip These

  • Fat burners
  • Testosterone boosters (OTC)
  • Detox teas and cleanses
The Golden Rule

If you can't explain in one sentence why you're taking a supplement — don't take it.
